Introduction

This page presents my applied and public-facing work beyond the core PhD research.

Engagement is where I explore how research ideas can become tools, frameworks, public knowledge, and practical interventions.

My current interests include designing an AI evaluation framework for vulnerable communities, thinking about AI assurance in social care and public service contexts, and translating developments in China’s AI landscape for wider audiences.

This section is designed as a growing showcase. It can hold project concepts, prototypes, video explainers, visual materials, reflections, and impact-oriented outputs.

Featured Directions

Three developing directions that shape my applied AI engagement work.

Project Direction

AI Evaluation Framework

Developing ways to evaluate whether AI tools are useful, safe, accessible, and appropriate for vulnerable communities.

Read More

This direction may include criteria such as trust, accessibility, fairness, explainability, user experience, safeguarding, and real-world usefulness.

Conceptual Direction

AI Assurance

Exploring how AI systems can be assessed, governed, and communicated in ways that support accountability and responsible use.

Read More

This includes thinking about risk, safety, evidence, transparency, professional responsibility, and how assurance practices can be meaningful in social and public service settings.

Public Knowledge

AI In China

Creating accessible explanations and reflections on China’s AI development, technology ecosystem, and social implications.

Read More

This direction can include short videos, explainers, public notes, policy reflections, industry observations, and comparisons between different AI development contexts.

Potential Impact

This work aims to translate research thinking into practical and public-facing outputs. It is especially concerned with how AI can be evaluated, explained, and responsibly adapted for sensitive social contexts.

  • Supporting more responsible AI design for vulnerable communities.
  • Developing an evaluation framework for AI tools in social contexts.
  • Improving public understanding of AI development and assurance.
  • Connecting academic research with practice-facing innovation.
